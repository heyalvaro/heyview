Context

Traffic wasn't
the problem.

CoolBrand CR is Costa Rica's premier luxury consignment boutique — collector watches, fine jewelry, rare handbags. The kind of store where a single piece can represent $5,000 to $20,000 and every sale starts with a conversation, not a checkout.

Their Shopify site had organic traffic. It also had zero inquiries. Visitors were landing and leaving without a word — not because they weren't interested, but because the design gave them no reason to trust the brand and no path to take action.

The brief was clear: this wasn't an SEO problem. It was a design problem. Build something that earns trust on first scroll, communicates luxury positioning, and routes intent into WhatsApp conversations. That's what Sinaí Alfaro designed — and what the numbers confirmed.

The diagnosis

Design is the product of a luxury brand's digital presence. When it looks generic, everything else looks cheap — no matter how good the inventory is. Fix the design, and the business follows.

Design & UX

Every decision
had a reason.

The redesign wasn't just visual — each structural choice was made to match how the brand operates and how its customers actually buy.

💬
WhatsApp over Cart

For high-value consignment items, a checkout button is the wrong interface. Customers need to ask about condition, provenance, or price before committing. A WhatsApp CTA on every product page — plus a persistent Floating Action Button across the entire site — means the path from "I'm interested" to "I'm talking to the store" is one tap.

◼
Dark Editorial Aesthetic

The brand's inventory competes with international luxury retailers in the feed. A light product-grid layout reads as commodity. A dark, high-contrast, editorial layout reads as boutique — with taste and authority. The palette and typography were chosen to feel like a collector's magazine, not a marketplace.

↕
Consignment Up Front

CoolBrand's model isn't just selling — it's acquiring inventory and fielding requests for specific pieces. "Sell or Consign Your Timepiece" and "Don't See What You Need?" became primary homepage sections, not footer links. The site now works as a two-way marketplace tool, not a passive catalog.

✦
Editorial Section Architecture

Nine distinct sections — Hero, Popular Collections, The Modern Collector's Guidebook, Curated Inventory, Inquiry Capture, Consignment Intake, Authentication Trust Signal, Client Testimonials, Private Events — each with a specific job. The structure guides a customer from discovery to trust to conversation without ever hitting a dead end.

⬚
Template System

Collection and product page templates were built so the client can upload new inventory without rebuilding layouts. The editorial header pattern — hero image + typographic overlay — was designed specifically so the team could maintain premium feel independently as inventory grows.

🔒
CRC Authenticated

Authentication and provenance are primary purchase objections in the secondary luxury market. Surfacing "CRC Authenticated" as a visible trust signal — not buried in an about page — addresses that objection at the moment a customer is building intent, not after they've already decided to leave.
