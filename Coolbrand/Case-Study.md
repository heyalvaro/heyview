## Context

CoolBrand CR is a luxury consignment boutique in Costa Rica specializing in collector watches, fine jewelry, and premium handbags — the kind of store where a single item can be worth thousands of dollars and every sale starts with a conversation.

Their existing Shopify site had traffic. That wasn't the problem. The problem was that the design felt generic — visually misaligned with the luxury positioning the brand was going for. It looked like dozens of other watch and jewelry stores online, with a cluttered layout, inconsistent category structure, and a standard e-commerce checkout experience that made no sense for high-value items where customers naturally want to talk before buying.

The result: **zero inquiries** despite meaningful organic traffic. Visitors were landing on the site and leaving without taking any action.

The brief was to redesign from the ground up — build something that matched the brand's identity (dark, editorial, collector-focused), made it easy for customers to explore curated inventory, and most importantly, channeled intent into WhatsApp conversations rather than into a shopping cart nobody was going to use.

## My Contribution

### Brand Direction & Visual Identity

Established an editorial luxury aesthetic: dark backgrounds, gold accent typography, high-contrast hero imagery, and a layout that feels closer to a collector's magazine than a traditional shop. The palette and tone were chosen to signal exclusivity without being cold — this is a boutique with personality.

### UX Architecture

Restructured the site into sections that match how CoolBrand's customers actually shop:

- **Hero** with a direct value prop: "Collector Favorites & Luxury Watches"
- **Popular Collections** — editorial entry points by category (watches, jewelry, handbags)
- **The Modern Collector's Guidebook** — an immersive editorial section that adds curatorial authority
- **Jewelry & More** — curated inventory with collection-style presentation
- **Don't See What You Need?** — a proactive inquiry capture for customers looking for specific pieces
- **Sell or Consign Your Timepiece** — intake form surfaced prominently, not buried
- **CRC Authenticated** — trust signal for authentication and provenance
- **Have Questions? / FAQ** — pre-purchase objection handling
- **Our Clients** — social proof with real customer photography
- **Private Events** — positions the brand as more than a store

### WhatsApp-First Conversion Design

The most consequential UX decision was replacing the standard Shopify cart flow with a WhatsApp-first purchase path. For a store selling luxury items at consignment prices, a "Buy Now" button that pushes customers through a checkout funnel is the wrong interface. Customers want to ask about condition, provenance, sizing, or negotiation before committing.

Two touch points were designed to drive these conversations:

1. **Per-product WhatsApp CTA** on every product detail page — positioned as the primary action above any cart option
2. **Floating Action Button (FAB)** — a persistent WhatsApp button visible across the entire site, so a customer who gets inspired while browsing can start a conversation from any page without hunting for contact info

This change directly addressed why the previous site generated no inquiries: it was asking customers to self-checkout on items they first needed to talk about.

### Collection & Filter Structure

Designed and implemented the collections taxonomy — men's watches, women's watches, jewelry, handbags, and accessories — with Shopify's native filtering surfaced consistently. Customers can get from the homepage to a filtered product grid in one click.

### Templates & Content System

Built and configured product and collection page templates so the client could upload new inventory without rebuilding layouts. The hero image + editorial header pattern used across collection pages (e.g., a section saying "Jewelry · Rare Selection") was specifically designed so the team could maintain a premium feel as they added content independently.

## Key Design Decisions Explained

### Why WhatsApp over cart
Luxury consignment doesn't convert through checkout flows. The item is unique, the price is negotiated, and the customer has trust questions before committing money. Putting WhatsApp at every conversion point — both sticky as a FAB and inline on product pages — means the path from "I'm interested" to "I'm talking to the store" is one tap. The old site made that path invisible.

### Why a dark editorial aesthetic
The brand's inventory competes with international luxury retailers on Instagram and in editorial contexts. A light, generic product-grid layout reads as a commodity store. A dark, high-contrast, editorial layout reads as a boutique with taste and authority — which is exactly how the client positions the store with their audience.

### Why consignment and inquiry sections are front and center
CoolBrand's business model isn't just selling — it's acquiring inventory through consignment and fielding requests for specific pieces. Surfacing "Sell or Consign" and "Don't See What You Need?" as primary sections (not footer links) converts the site from a passive catalog into a two-way marketplace tool.
